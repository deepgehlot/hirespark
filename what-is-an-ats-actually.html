RESUME TIPS

Here’s what an ATS actually is explained by someone who rewrites resumes everyday

Here’s the real answer in normal-person language.

What is an ATS?

ATS = the software companies use to store, search, and organize resumes. That’s literally it. Nothing magical. Nothing “AI.” More like a glorified filing cabinet with a search bar.

Let me explain it the way I explain it to clients:

1. It searches for exact words. That’s all.

Not “similar words.” Not “it understands the meaning.” It’s dumb.

Example:
Job post wants “SQL and Tableau.”
Your resume says:
• “Data visualization”
• “Querying experience”

The ATS goes: “I don’t see SQL. I don’t see Tableau.”
→ And you disappear into page 7 of the candidate list.

Recruiters never see you not because you’re unqualified, but because the system is basically a brick.

2. Pretty templates break it.

I know Canva resumes look nice. I get why people use them. But ATS reads them like a blindfolded toddler with a keyboard.

Example:
You put your phone number inside a little icon bubble?
ATS: “??? No phone number found.”

Same with email, job titles, half your experience it just gets scrambled.

3. It’s not rejecting you. It’s just sorting people.

Most rejections aren’t “rejections.” It’s literally:

Recruiter: “Show me everyone with Python.”
ATS: (shows only resumes containing the exact word Python).

If the keyword isn’t there? You don’t exist.

4. A lot of ATS systems are ancient.

Some companies are using software older than the iPhone 5. That’s why things like:

  • tables
  • text boxes
  • fancy shapes
  • icons
  • weird fonts

…make them freak out.

Example: you put your job title inside a text box.
ATS reads it as an image.
→ Your job title disappears.
→ Recruiter sees a blank header and moves on.

Not your fault. The tech is just outdated as hell.

5. How to avoid all this (super simple).

Not overthinking it. Just:

  • keep the layout clean
  • use the same wording the job post uses
  • avoid icons + text boxes
  • keep it readable for actual humans

You’re not “tricking” anything. You’re just making sure the system doesn’t choke on formatting.
